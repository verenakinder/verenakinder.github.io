Has the EU Truly Become a Security Provider? Institutional Progress and Political Limits in European Defence

This working paper examines whether the European Union (EU) can plausibly be understood as a security provider after Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine and the adoption of the Strategic Compass in 2022.

Responsibility in the Shadows: The Hmong in Laos and the Responsibility Gap of Covert Proxy Interventions

This working paper examines structural limitations of international law, especially the law of state responsibility, in the context of covert proxy interventions. Using the Hmong case in Laos as an illustrative example, it argues that the central limitation lies not only in attribution under Article 8 of the Articles on State Responsibility, but also in the state-centred implementation architecture of international responsibility.
